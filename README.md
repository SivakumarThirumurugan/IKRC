## 🚀 Project Overview

This repository documents the mechanical design and engineering management work carried out for the **Indian Kart Racing Championship (IKRC) 2021**, a national-level collegiate design challenge organized by Titan Autocorp.

As **Design Team Leader**, I directed the full mechanical engineering lifecycle of a competition racing kart — from interpreting IKRC technical regulations through to producing a manufacturable, competition-ready vehicle design using Siemens NX.

---

## 🛠️ Technical Stack

| Tool | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Siemens NX | 3D parametric modeling, assembly design, and drafting |
| Siemens NX Motion | Kinematic simulation and dynamic load analysis |
| Siemens NX CAE | Structural feasibility and stress distribution checks |

---

## ⚙️ Core Responsibilities

**Engineering Leadership**
Led the mechanical design team through the complete product development cycle, ensuring all design decisions adhered to IKRC technical and safety regulations.

**Chassis & Systems Design**
Executed the primary mechanical design of the racing kart with focus on structural integrity, driver ergonomics, and weight optimization within competition constraints.

**Cross-Functional Coordination**
Coordinated between fabrication, assembly, and testing units to ensure CAD models translated accurately into physical prototypes with correct manufacturing tolerances.

**Design Validation**
Monitored mechanical feasibility and tolerance stack-ups to ensure the kart chassis could withstand the dynamic loads of competitive kart racing.

---

## 📐 Key Engineering Decisions

- **Frame Geometry** — Optimized wheelbase and track width for cornering stability within IKRC class regulations
- **Weight Distribution** — Driver and powertrain positioning tuned for balanced front/rear weight ratio
- **Ergonomics** — Seat and pedal layout designed for driver fit across standard percentile ranges
- **Tolerance Management** — Critical bearing and spindle interfaces designed with GD&T-compliant clearances
